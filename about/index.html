I am a Software Engineer at TikTok US, building distributed systems and the infrastructure that powers modern AI systems.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Software Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University. At CMU, I worked with Prof. Leonardo Sousa at the Distributed Systems Research Group, where my work focused on distributed systems, cloud-native infrastructure, and scalable software architecture.

Before Carnegie Mellon, I worked as a software engineer at TikTok China, where I designed and built backend systems, distributed services, and platform infrastructure for one of the world’s largest e-commerce platforms.

My work has focused on distributed systems, with a growing emphasis on AI infrastructure and intelligent systems. Beyond work, I enjoy building open-source software and sharing what I learn through technical writing.

Experience

Northeast Normal University

During my undergraduate studies at Northeast Normal University, I joined the Intelligent Information Processing Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Junping Zhou, where I conducted research in graph algorithms and combinatorial optimization, with a focus on the Top-K Clique problem.

Beyond research, engineering projects and open-source contributions sparked my interest in software engineering, laying the foundation for my journey into distributed systems.

TikTok China

I began my career at TikTok China on the Global E-Commerce Fulfillment team, where I designed and built distributed backend systems that supported the rapid growth of one of the world’s largest e-commerce platforms as it scaled from approximately 56K to over 10 million daily orders.

As the platform evolved, I led architectural improvements that enabled faster product iteration while keeping the system scalable, reliable, and maintainable. My contributions were recognized with an Exceed Expectations (E) performance rating and a fast-track promotion to Software Engineer II.

More importantly, it was where I developed my foundation in infrastructure engineering, learning how scalable distributed systems are designed, built, and operated in real production environments.

Carnegie Mellon University

At Carnegie Mellon University, I joined the Distributed Systems Research Group under the supervision of Prof. Leonardo Sousa, where I explored the principles behind reliable and scalable distributed systems through both research and engineering.

This research experience strengthened my theoretical foundation in distributed systems while shaping a more systematic approach to infrastructure engineering, complementing the practical experience I had gained in industry.

TikTok US

I am currently a Software Engineer at TikTok US, building distributed backend systems and platform infrastructure.

As we build the next generation of AI-powered products, we are exploring runtime infrastructure for long-running, stateful AI workflows, including durable execution, production observability, and continuous evaluation.
